Express Entry Draw #426 Results: 500 Invited at CRS 392

Express Entry Draw #426: Canada Invites 500 Senior Managers with Canadian Work Experience

Draw Date: July 10, 2026  |  Draw Type: Senior Managers with Canadian Work Experience (2026-Version 1)  |  Invitations Issued: 500  |  CRS Cutoff: 392

Draw Summary: What Happened on July 10, 2026

Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) conducted Express Entry Draw #426 on July 10, 2026, issuing 500 Invitations to Apply (ITAs) to candidates in the senior managers category with verified Canadian work experience. This was a category-based selection draw targeting individuals classified under NOC Major Group 00—senior managers and officials who have held leadership roles across a broad range of industries in Canada.

The minimum Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score required to receive an invitation was 392, a figure that signals meaningful accessibility for a category-based draw of this type. Candidates at or above this threshold who met the eligibility criteria would have received their ITA automatically on July 10, 2026. Recipients now have a strict 60-day window to compile and submit a complete permanent residence application to IRCC—a deadline that should not be taken lightly.

What a CRS Cutoff of 392 Means for Applicants in the Pool

A cutoff of 392 is a meaningful benchmark in the context of category-based draws. Unlike all-program draws, where CRS scores have historically hovered in the 470s to 490s range, category-based draws frequently pull candidates with lower scores because eligibility is filtered first by occupation and work experience, not solely by overall CRS rank.

For senior managers in the Express Entry pool, a score of 392 is attainable through a combination of age, education, language proficiency, and Canadian work experience factors. Candidates who have been working in senior management roles in Canada and have been accumulating CRS points over time may find this cutoff well within reach, particularly if they hold a Canadian post-secondary credential or have a spouse with strong language scores.

If your CRS score sits at exactly 392 or higher and you are classified under NOC Major Group 00 with qualifying Canadian work experience, you should log into your IRCC account immediately to confirm whether an ITA was issued to your profile.

Trend Context: How Does This Compare to Recent Senior Manager Draws?

Category-based draws targeting senior managers have been a recurring feature of Canada's Express Entry strategy since IRCC expanded its category-based selection authority under Bill C-19 in 2022. These draws reflect Canada's deliberate effort to recruit experienced executive and management talent to address gaps in organizational leadership across sectors including healthcare administration, financial services, technology, and construction management.

The 500 invitations issued in Draw #426 represent a focused, targeted pull rather than a broad sweep of the pool. Previous senior manager draws have varied in size, with some issuing as few as 300 ITAs and others reaching 750 or more depending on IRCC's intake targets and pool composition at the time of the draw. A cutoff of 392 suggests a healthy volume of eligible candidates in the pool whose scores cluster around that range, and that IRCC did not need to reach deeply below the 400-point mark to fill its 500-invitation quota.

This draw is identified as the 2026-Version 1 of the senior managers category, indicating it is the first draw of this specific type conducted in the 2026 calendar year, and further rounds targeting this category are likely to follow later in the year.

Who Specifically Benefits from Draw #426

This draw is designed for a precise profile of Express Entry candidate. To have been eligible for Draw #426, you must meet all of the following criteria:

  • Hold a valid Express Entry profile in the pool at the time of the draw (July 10, 2026)
  • Be classified under NOC Major Group 00, which includes senior government managers, senior managers in financial and communications services, senior managers in trade, broadcasting, and other services, and senior managers in health, education, social and community services
  • Have accumulated qualifying Canadian work experience in a NOC 00 occupation within the required timeframe
  • Have a CRS score of 392 or higher at the time of the draw

If you are a Chief Executive Officer, a Vice President, a Director-General, a hospital administrator, or a senior public service executive who has been working in Canada and maintaining an active Express Entry profile, this draw was built for candidates like you.

What Applicants Just Below the Cutoff Should Do Right Now

If your CRS score falls between 370 and 391, you were not captured in this round—but that does not mean your pathway to permanent residence is closed. Here is what you should do immediately:

  • Review your CRS breakdown carefully. Identify which factors are dragging your score down. Common areas for improvement include secondary language scores (French), spouse or common-law partner factors, and arranged employment.
  • Retake language tests if necessary. Even a one-point improvement across CLB bands in your IELTS or CELPIP results can meaningfully increase your CRS score.
  • Consider a provincial nominee program (PNP). A provincial nomination adds 600 points to your CRS score and virtually guarantees an ITA in a subsequent all-program draw. Several provinces actively target senior managers through their streams.
