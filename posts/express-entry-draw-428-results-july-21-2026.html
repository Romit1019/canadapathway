Express Entry Draw #428 Results: 2000 Invited at CRS 516

Express Entry Draw #428: Canada Issues 2,000 Invitations to Canadian Experience Class Candidates

Draw Date: July 21, 2026  |  Draw Type: Canadian Experience Class (CEC)  |  Invitations Issued: 2,000  |  CRS Cutoff: 516

Draw Summary: What Happened

Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) conducted Express Entry Draw #428 on July 21, 2026, issuing 2,000 Invitations to Apply (ITAs) exclusively to candidates in the Canadian Experience Class stream. The minimum Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score required to receive an invitation was 516.

This was a CEC-specific draw, meaning only candidates who have accumulated eligible skilled work experience inside Canada were considered. Candidates in the Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP) or Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP) pools were not included in this round, regardless of their CRS score. With 2,000 ITAs on the table, this draw represents a moderately sized round that continues Canada's targeted approach to Express Entry selections in 2026.

What a CRS Cutoff of 516 Means for Applicants in the Pool

A cutoff of 516 is a demanding benchmark. To put it in perspective, reaching 516 in the CEC category typically requires a combination of strong Canadian work experience, high language test scores — ideally CLB 9 or above in all four abilities — post-secondary education completed in Canada or assessed as equivalent, and ideally more than one year of skilled work experience in Canada.

For candidates currently sitting in the Express Entry pool with a CEC profile, this cutoff means the competition remains stiff. If your CRS score falls below 516 today, you did not receive an invitation in this round. However, this does not mean your pathway to permanent residence is closed. It means your profile remains active in the pool and could be selected in a future draw — provided you either wait for the cutoff to shift downward or take deliberate steps to increase your score before your profile expires.

It is also worth noting that CEC candidates who have already been in the pool for several months without receiving an ITA may be approaching their 12-month profile expiry. If that applies to you, renewing your profile promptly is essential to avoid losing your place in the pool entirely.

Trend Context: How Draw #428 Compares to Recent CEC Rounds

Canadian Experience Class draws have consistently produced competitive CRS cutoffs throughout 2025 and into 2026. The 516 cutoff seen in Draw #428 is in line with the elevated score requirements that have characterized CEC-specific rounds over the past several months, reflecting continued demand for candidates with proven Canadian work experience and limited pool volume at the top of the CRS distribution.

When IRCC targets the CEC specifically rather than conducting an all-program draw, cutoffs tend to be higher because the eligible pool is narrower. All-program draws pull from a broader range of candidates and typically produce lower cutoffs, but CEC-only draws reward those who have already built a life and career in Canada. The 2,000 ITAs issued in this round is a consistent volume for CEC draws, suggesting IRCC is maintaining a steady pace of invitations for this category without significantly expanding or contracting allocations.

Who Benefits Most From Draw #428

The candidates who benefited directly from Draw #428 are those who scored 516 or above on the CRS scale and held an active Canadian Experience Class profile on July 21, 2026. These individuals typically share a common profile: they have completed at least one year of NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3 work experience in Canada within the past three years, achieved high scores on either the IELTS or CELPIP (or TEF Canada for French-language applicants), and may hold a Canadian educational credential that boosted their human capital score.

French-speaking CEC candidates also stand to benefit significantly from CEC draws. French-language proficiency awards up to 50 additional CRS points for bilingual candidates, which can be the difference between sitting below the cutoff and receiving an invitation. If you have strong French skills alongside your English proficiency, ensuring your TEF Canada scores are included in your profile is a high-value action.

What Applicants Just Below the Cutoff Should Do Right Now

If your CRS score is in the range of 500 to 515, you were close — but not close enough in this round. Here is what you should do immediately:

  • Retake your language test. Moving from CLB 8 to CLB 9 in even one language ability can add meaningful points to your CRS score. This is consistently the fastest and most controllable way to increase your score.
  • Pursue a provincial nomination. A nomination through a Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) aligned with Express Entry adds 600 points to your CRS score — effectively guaranteeing an ITA in the next available round. Research which provinces are currently drawing candidates from the Express Entry pool with profiles matching yours.
  • Secure a valid job offer. A qualifying job offer at NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3 from a Canadian employer can add 50 to 200 additional CRS points depending on the NOC code.
  • Accumulate additional Canadian work experience. If you are approaching the one-year mark in a second eligible occupation, that additional experience category can push your score higher.
  • Keep your profile updated and active. An outdated profile can cost you points. Ensure your job title, language scores, and educational credentials reflect your current situation.

Draw #428 is a reminder that the path to Canadian permanent residence through the CEC rewards those who invest in building a competitive profile — not just those who wait
