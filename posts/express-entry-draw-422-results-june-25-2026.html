Express Entry Draw #422: Canada Issues 4,000 ITAs to Healthcare and Social Services Workers

Draw Date: June 25, 2026  |  Draw Type: Healthcare and Social Services Occupations  |  Invitations Issued: 4,000  |  CRS Cutoff: 475

Draw Summary: What Happened

Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) conducted Express Entry Draw #422 on June 25, 2026, issuing 4,000 Invitations to Apply (ITAs) exclusively to candidates in eligible Healthcare and Social Services occupations. This is the third category-based draw of 2026 targeting this sector, reflecting Canada's continued prioritization of healthcare talent to address persistent workforce shortages across provinces and territories.

The minimum Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) cutoff for this round landed at 475 points. Any eligible candidate in the healthcare and social services category who held a CRS score of 475 or higher as of June 25, 2026, and who had a valid Express Entry profile, was issued an ITA to apply for permanent residence. Candidates with tied scores at exactly 475 were assessed using the tie-breaking rule based on profile submission date, with earlier-submitted profiles receiving priority.

With 4,000 invitations on the table, this draw represents a substantial intake for a single category-based round, signalling that IRCC is moving aggressively to meet its annual immigration targets within the healthcare sector.

What a 475 CRS Cutoff Means for Applicants in the Pool

A cutoff of 475 sits in a moderately competitive range for category-based draws. Unlike general rounds, which can push cutoffs into the 490s and above, healthcare-specific draws tend to operate at lower thresholds because the eligible candidate pool is more narrowly defined by occupation type rather than overall score ranking.

For candidates currently in the Express Entry pool with eligible healthcare or social services occupations, a score of 475 is an achievable but not trivial benchmark. The CRS system awards points across four core areas: core human capital factors (age, education, language, and Canadian work experience), spouse or partner factors, skill transferability, and additional points such as a provincial nomination or job offer. Reaching 475 typically requires a combination of strong language results, post-secondary education at the bachelor's level or above, and meaningful skilled work experience.

Candidates sitting at 476 or higher can consider themselves well-positioned for future rounds in this category, assuming they maintain an active and up-to-date Express Entry profile.

Trend Context: How Draw #422 Compares to Recent Healthcare Rounds

Draw #422 is the third healthcare and social services draw of 2026, following the same category introduced under Canada's category-based selection framework. Comparing across recent rounds in this category, the 475 cutoff is consistent with the competitive range that has defined healthcare draws throughout 2025 and into 2026. These draws have generally held cutoffs between 470 and 485, reflecting a pool that is growing as more healthcare professionals build their Express Entry profiles but has not yet become oversaturated.

The volume of 4,000 invitations also aligns with IRCC's pattern of issuing large batches in occupation-specific rounds, a strategy designed to maximize labour market impact while keeping processing pipelines manageable. Taken together, Draw #422 confirms that healthcare category draws remain one of the most accessible pathways for skilled workers in this sector, with lower cutoffs and higher invitation volumes than all-program general rounds.

Who Specifically Benefits from Draw #422

This draw benefits Express Entry candidates whose primary occupation falls within IRCC's designated Healthcare and Social Services category. Eligible NOC codes under the 2021 NOC system include roles such as:

  • Registered nurses and licensed practical nurses
  • Physicians, surgeons, and specialist doctors
  • Pharmacists and pharmacy technicians
  • Medical laboratory technologists and technicians
  • Physiotherapists, occupational therapists, and related rehabilitation professionals
  • Social workers and community service workers
  • Personal support workers and home health aides in eligible classifications
  • Dental hygienists and dental therapists

Candidates in these roles who had active profiles in the Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP), the Canadian Experience Class (CEC), or the Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP) within Express Entry, and who scored at or above 475, were eligible to receive an ITA in this draw.

What Applicants Just Below 475 Should Do Right Now

If your CRS score falls below 475, you were not invited in Draw #422 — but that does not mean permanent residence is out of reach. There are several concrete actions you can take to push your score above the cutoff before the next healthcare draw:

  1. Retake your language test. Language scores under CLB 9 or 10 leave significant CRS points on the table. Improving from CLB 8 to CLB 9 in all four abilities can add 30 or more points to your score.
  2. Pursue a Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) nomination. A valid provincial nomination adds 600 points to your CRS score, virtually guaranteeing an ITA in the next available round. Several provinces actively target healthcare workers through dedicated streams.
  3. Secure a valid job offer. A qualifying job offer from a Canadian employer at NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3 can add 50 to 200 points depending on the position.
  4. Accumulate additional Canadian work experience. If you are already working in Canada, additional months of skilled Canadian experience can push your score incrementally higher
