Express Entry Draw #423 Results: 534 Invited at CRS 708

Express Entry Draw #423: Canada Issues 534 Invitations to Provincial Nominees with 708 CRS Cutoff

Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) held Express Entry Draw #423 on July 6, 2026, issuing 534 Invitations to Apply (ITAs) exclusively to candidates in the Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) category. The minimum CRS score required to receive an invitation was 708, one of the highest thresholds seen in recent PNP-specific rounds.

Draw Summary: What Happened

Draw #423 was a targeted PNP round, meaning only Express Entry candidates who already hold a valid provincial or territorial nomination were eligible to receive an invitation. A total of 534 ITAs were issued — a relatively modest volume compared to some broader all-program draws — reflecting IRCC's continued use of category-based and program-specific selection to manage the composition of incoming permanent residents.

The CRS cutoff of 708 is significant. At first glance, it may seem alarmingly high, but it is important to understand the mechanics behind PNP scoring before drawing conclusions about your own eligibility.

What a 708 CRS Cutoff Actually Means for PNP Candidates

Provincial nominations carry an automatic 600-point boost to a candidate's Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score. This means that the 708 cutoff in Draw #423 effectively required candidates to have an underlying human capital score — based on factors like age, education, language ability, and Canadian work experience — of approximately 108 points or higher before the provincial nomination was applied.

To put that in perspective, a score of 108 from human capital factors alone is achievable for a wide range of profiles. A candidate in their late 20s or early 30s with a post-secondary diploma, strong English or French language scores (CLB 7 or higher), and a job offer or modest work experience can realistically reach this threshold. This means the true barrier to receiving an ITA in this draw was not an extraordinary human capital profile — it was holding the provincial nomination itself.

If you do not currently hold a provincial nomination, this draw is entirely separate from your Express Entry standing. Your CRS score in the pool, whether it is 400 or 550, had no bearing on Draw #423. PNP draws operate in a lane of their own.

How Draw #423 Compares to Recent PNP Rounds

PNP-specific Express Entry draws have become a recurring feature of IRCC's draw schedule. Recent rounds of this type have typically featured CRS cutoffs in the 700 to 720 range, reflecting the consistent 600-point nomination bonus layered onto varying human capital scores. A cutoff of 708 is firmly within the expected range for this draw category and does not indicate a tightening of PNP selection in any alarming sense.

The invitation volume of 534 is on the lower end for a standalone PNP draw. Some previous PNP rounds have issued upward of 700 to 1,000 invitations. The smaller volume in this round may suggest a temporary reduction in the PNP allocation within IRCC's annual targets, or simply reflect the size of the eligible pool at the time of the draw. Candidates waiting on provincial nominations should not read this as a long-term contraction of PNP pathways.

Who Specifically Benefits From This Draw

Draw #423 directly benefits a specific and narrow group of applicants:

  • Express Entry candidates who received a valid provincial or territorial nomination before July 6, 2026
  • Candidates whose combined CRS score — human capital plus the 600-point PNP bonus — reached or exceeded 708
  • Individuals nominated through streams tied to the Federal Skilled Worker Program, Federal Skilled Trades Program, or Canadian Experience Class under the Express Entry-aligned PNP streams

If you are in this group and your score met the cutoff, you should have received your ITA and now have 60 days to submit a complete application for permanent residence. Ensure your documents — including police certificates, medical exams, and language test results — are current and ready to upload promptly.

What Applicants Just Below the Cutoff Should Do Now

If you hold a provincial nomination and your CRS score came in just below 708, your most immediate priority is identifying whether you can increase your human capital score before the next PNP draw. Focus on the factors most within your control:

  • Retake language tests — Moving from CLB 8 to CLB 9 in English or French can add meaningful points to your score
  • Verify your education assessment — Ensure your Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) accurately reflects your highest level of study
  • Accumulate additional Canadian work experience — Each additional year of skilled Canadian work experience adds CRS points
  • Check your profile completeness — Errors or missing information in your Express Entry profile can artificially suppress your score

If you do not yet hold a provincial nomination, now is the time to research province-specific streams aligned with your occupation. Ontario, British Columbia, Alberta, and Nova Scotia all operate active Express Entry-aligned streams with regular intakes. Securing a nomination is the single most impactful action you can take to advance in the Express Entry pool.
