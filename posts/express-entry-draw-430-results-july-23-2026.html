Express Entry Draw #430 Results: 4 Invited at CRS 368

Express Entry Draw #430: Canada Issues 4 ITAs to Skilled Military Recruits

Draw Date: July 23, 2026  |  Draw Type: Skilled Military Recruits, 2026-Version 1  |  Invitations Issued: 4  |  CRS Cutoff: 368

Draw Summary: What Happened

Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) conducted Express Entry Draw #430 on July 23, 2026, issuing just 4 Invitations to Apply (ITAs) under the new Skilled Military Recruits category, designated as the 2026-Version 1 iteration of this program stream. The minimum Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score required to receive an invitation was 368.

This draw is notable for its exceptionally limited scope. With only four candidates selected, Draw #430 ranks among the smallest Express Entry rounds ever conducted. Category-based draws targeting niche occupational or credential groups are not unusual, but a draw of this size signals a highly targeted and controlled selection approach rather than a broad intake effort. The Skilled Military Recruits category itself is a newer addition to the category-based selection framework, designed to attract foreign nationals with verified military service backgrounds who can contribute to Canadian defence and security-adjacent industries.

What a CRS Cutoff of 368 Means for Applicants in the Pool

A CRS cutoff of 368 is meaningfully lower than the scores typically required in general draws or even many other category-based rounds. For context, all-program draws in recent months have generally demanded CRS scores well above 500, and many category-specific draws have seen cutoffs ranging from the low 400s to the mid-500s depending on the stream.

A cutoff of 368 indicates that the pool of eligible Skilled Military Recruits candidates in the Express Entry system is relatively small. When IRCC sets a low cutoff, it is often because the number of qualifying candidates is limited — meaning the agency must lower the threshold to reach even a small target invitation count. In practical terms, this means that military-credentialed candidates do not need an exceptionally high CRS score to be competitive within this specific category, provided they meet all underlying eligibility criteria.

For general pool candidates without military backgrounds, this draw has no direct impact on their standing or CRS requirements. The selection pool for category-based draws is separate from the ranking used in all-program rounds.

Trend Context: How This Compares to Other Skilled Military Recruits Draws

As the first draw designated under the 2026-Version 1 label for the Skilled Military Recruits category, Draw #430 establishes an early benchmark for this stream in the current program year. The label "2026-Version 1" suggests IRCC may issue subsequent iterations of this category draw throughout 2026, potentially with adjusted invitation volumes or updated eligibility criteria.

The decision to issue only 4 ITAs suggests IRCC is either stress-testing the category's eligibility framework, responding to a limited number of verified qualifying profiles currently active in the pool, or managing intake deliberately while the category matures. Applicants following this stream should watch closely for subsequent draws under this category, as invitation volumes and CRS thresholds may shift considerably as the program gains traction and more candidates enter the pool with military credentials properly documented.

Who Specifically Benefits from Draw #430

The four individuals who received ITAs in this draw are candidates who:

  • Had an active Express Entry profile at the time of the draw on July 23, 2026
  • Met the eligibility criteria for the Skilled Military Recruits category as defined under the 2026-Version 1 framework
  • Held a CRS score at or above 368 at the time of selection
  • Had their military credentials and service history accurately reflected in their profile

Eligible candidates typically include foreign nationals with documented military service in recognized armed forces, whose skills and experience align with occupations in demand in Canada. If you hold military experience and have not yet confirmed your eligibility under this category, now is the time to review IRCC's official category criteria and ensure your profile is fully updated.

What Applicants Just Below the Cutoff Should Do Now

If your CRS score falls below 368 and you believe you may qualify for the Skilled Military Recruits category, do not wait passively. Here is what you should do immediately:

  1. Audit your Express Entry profile. Ensure every element of your profile — language scores, education credentials, work history, and military service records — is accurately entered. Errors or missing data can suppress your CRS score unnecessarily.
  2. Retake language tests if eligible. Improving your IELTS or CELPIP score by even one CLB level can meaningfully boost your CRS total.
  3. Pursue a provincial nomination. A valid provincial nomination adds 600 CRS points, virtually guaranteeing an ITA in any future draw regardless of category cutoffs.
  4. Verify your category eligibility. Confirm with a regulated immigration consultant or lawyer that your military background qualifies under the 2026-Version 1 criteria for this specific stream.
  5. Monitor upcoming draws. Given that this is the first draw of its version year, subsequent Skilled Military Recruits draws are likely. A higher invitation volume in future rounds could bring the cutoff even lower.
