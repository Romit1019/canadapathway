Express Entry Draw #424 Results: 2000 Invited at CRS 517

Express Entry Draw #424: Canada Issues 2,000 Invitations to CEC Candidates with CRS of 517

Draw Date: July 7, 2026  |  Draw Type: Canadian Experience Class  |  Invitations Issued: 2,000  |  CRS Cutoff: 517

What Happened in Draw #424

Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) conducted Express Entry Draw #424 on July 7, 2026, issuing 2,000 Invitations to Apply (ITAs) exclusively to candidates in the Canadian Experience Class (CEC) stream. The minimum Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score required to receive an invitation was 517.

This was a program-specific draw targeting only CEC-eligible candidates — meaning applicants who have accumulated at least one year of skilled work experience in Canada under NOC TEER categories 0, 1, 2, or 3 within the past three years. Candidates in other streams, such as the Federal Skilled Worker (FSW) or Federal Skilled Trades (FST) programs, were not eligible for this round regardless of their CRS score.

With 2,000 ITAs issued, this draw represents a moderately sized round for the CEC stream, maintaining consistent volume for Canadian-based workers seeking permanent residency through their domestic experience.

What a 517 Cutoff Means for Applicants in the Pool

A CRS cutoff of 517 is a high threshold that reflects the competitive nature of the Canadian Experience Class pool. To put this in perspective, a score of 517 typically requires a combination of strong performance across multiple CRS factors, including:

  • Age: Candidates in their late 20s to early 30s receive maximum age points
  • Language proficiency: CLB 9 or higher in both official languages significantly boosts scores
  • Canadian work experience: Three or more years of eligible Canadian experience provides additional points
  • Education: A Canadian post-secondary credential or a foreign credential with an Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) at the bachelor's level or higher contributes meaningfully
  • Spouse or partner factors: A spouse with strong language scores and Canadian work experience can add supplementary points

Candidates sitting at or above 517 in the CEC pool who did not receive an ITA in this round should review their profile to confirm their CEC eligibility is correctly declared, as IRCC selects candidates in rank order based on the tie-breaking rule of the earliest profile submission date.

How Draw #424 Compares to Recent CEC Draws

CEC-specific draws have been a regular feature of Canada's Express Entry calendar, typically alternating with all-program draws and category-based selection rounds. A cutoff of 517 places Draw #424 within a competitive but consistent range for CEC rounds in 2025 and 2026, where scores have generally fluctuated between 508 and 525 depending on pool size and draw volume.

When IRCC issues larger draws — such as rounds with 3,000 or more ITAs — the cutoff tends to drop, bringing more candidates into range. A 2,000-ITA draw at 517 suggests that the CEC pool remains well-stocked with high-scoring candidates, keeping cutoffs elevated. Applicants should not expect a dramatic drop in the cutoff in the near term unless IRCC significantly increases draw volumes.

Who Benefits Most from This Draw

Draw #424 is a direct win for a specific group of candidates:

  • Temporary foreign workers and international graduates who have been living and working in Canada and have built up sufficient CRS points through a combination of Canadian experience, strong English or French scores, and post-secondary credentials
  • PGWP holders (Post-Graduation Work Permit) who completed a Canadian degree and have since accumulated one or more years of NOC TEER 0–3 work experience
  • Bilingual candidates who have demonstrated proficiency in both English and French, earning additional CRS points through the bilingual language bonus
  • Candidates with three or more years of Canadian work experience, who receive the maximum CEC work experience points under the CRS grid

If your CRS score is 517 or above and your profile is active and CEC-eligible, this draw may have generated your ITA. Check your IRCC account immediately, as you will have 60 days from the date of invitation to submit a complete permanent residence application.

What to Do If Your Score Is Below 517

If your CRS score falls short of the 517 cutoff, there are concrete steps you can take right now to improve your competitiveness before the next CEC-specific draw:

  1. Retake your language test. Moving from CLB 8 to CLB 9 in English or French can add 20 to 30+ CRS points depending on your profile. Book your IELTS General Training or CELPIP exam and prepare strategically for the speaking and writing components, where candidates most often lose points.
