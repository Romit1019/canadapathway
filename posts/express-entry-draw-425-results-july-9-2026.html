Express Entry Draw #425 Results: 5000 Invited at CRS 420

Express Entry Draw #425: Canada Issues 5,000 Invitations to French-Language Candidates

Draw Date: July 9, 2026  |  Draw Type: French-Language Proficiency  |  Invitations Issued: 5,000  |  CRS Cutoff: 420

Draw Summary: What Happened

Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) conducted Express Entry Draw #425 on July 9, 2026, issuing 5,000 Invitations to Apply (ITAs) exclusively to candidates with demonstrated French-language proficiency. The Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) cutoff score for this round landed at 420 points, making it the second French-language proficiency draw of 2026 and one of the more accessible rounds of this category in recent memory.

This draw falls under Canada's targeted category-based selection strategy, which prioritizes French-speaking immigrants to support the government's commitment to Francophone minority communities outside Quebec. Candidates eligible for this draw must have submitted valid results from either the TEF Canada or TCF Canada language test, achieving a minimum Canadian Language Benchmark (CLB) level of 7 in all four language abilities: speaking, listening, reading, and writing.

What a 420 CRS Cutoff Means for Applicants in the Pool

A cutoff of 420 is a notably low threshold for any Express Entry draw, and that is especially significant for French-language candidates who may not otherwise reach the higher scores typically required in All-Program rounds. To put this in perspective, general All-Program draws in 2025 and 2026 have frequently cleared scores in the 480 to 510 range, meaning tens of thousands of candidates in the pool would not qualify under those draws regardless of their Francophone status.

At 420, a meaningful portion of the French-proficient pool becomes eligible. Candidates can realistically reach this score through a combination of factors including age, education, work experience, and language scores in English or French — even without a valid job offer or a Canadian credential. For example, a candidate in their late twenties with a bachelor's degree, three or more years of skilled work experience, and strong CLB 9 scores in French can comfortably approach or exceed 420 without any additional bonus points.

The 5,000 invitations issued also signals that IRCC is drawing deeply into the French-language pool, suggesting a robust pipeline of Francophone applicants is being processed and that Canada continues to aggressively pursue its Francophone immigration targets.

Trend Context: How Draw #425 Compares to Recent French-Language Rounds

The first French-language proficiency draw of 2026 (Draw #425 being the second) set a baseline for what applicants could expect this year. Historically, French-language draws have carried CRS cutoffs ranging from as low as 336 in earlier rounds to well above 470 during periods of lower Francophone pool volume. A cutoff of 420 places this draw on the more generous end of the spectrum, suggesting either a larger-than-usual pool of eligible Francophone candidates or a deliberate policy decision to maximize intake volume.

The 5,000 ITA figure is also a high-volume allocation for a targeted category draw, reinforcing Canada's legislative targets under the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act to grow Francophone communities outside Quebec to at least 4.4% of the total immigrant population by 2023 — a goal that continues to drive policy into 2026.

Who Specifically Benefits From This Draw

Draw #425 is most advantageous for the following candidate profiles:

  • Francophone candidates with CLB 7 or higher in French who have been sitting in the Express Entry pool with scores between 420 and 450 and had not previously received an ITA.
  • Candidates with post-secondary education from a foreign institution who may lack the Canadian study bonus but compensate with strong French scores and several years of work experience.
  • Mid-career applicants aged 25 to 35 who benefit from near-maximum age points and have accumulated sufficient work experience in a skilled NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3 occupation.
  • Bilingual candidates who have strong scores in both English and French, as the combination of first and second official language proficiency can add meaningful CRS points.
  • Applicants with a provincial nomination who also qualify on French-language grounds, though these candidates typically already hold 600-point CRS scores and would likely have been captured in earlier rounds.

What Applicants Just Below 420 Should Do Right Now

If your current CRS score falls between 400 and 419, you are within striking distance of the cutoff and should take immediate, targeted action to close that gap before the next French-language draw.

First, review your French test scores. If you wrote TEF Canada or TCF Canada more than two years ago or scored at CLB 7, retaking the exam and achieving CLB 9 or higher in all four abilities could add up to 50 or more CRS points depending on your profile. Second, verify your education credentials are accurately reflected in your profile with an Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) from a designated organization. An overlooked credential or an improperly categorized degree can cost you valuable points. Third, if you have a sibling, spouse, or common-law partner who is a Canadian citizen or permanent resident, ensure the adaptability points for that connection are claimed in your profile.
