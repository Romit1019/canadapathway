Express Entry Draw #429 Results: 5000 Invited at CRS 399

Express Entry Draw #429: Canada Issues 5,000 ITAs to French-Language Proficiency Candidates with 399 CRS Cutoff

Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) conducted Express Entry Draw #429 on July 22, 2026, targeting candidates with French-language proficiency. The draw issued 5,000 Invitations to Apply (ITAs) with a CRS cutoff score of 399, making it one of the most accessible French-language draws on record and a significant opportunity for Francophone applicants sitting in the Express Entry pool.

Draw Summary: What Happened

Draw #429 is classified as a French-Language Proficiency draw (2026 Version 2), meaning it was exclusively open to Express Entry candidates who meet IRCC's criteria for French-language ability. To qualify for this category, candidates must demonstrate strong proficiency in French, typically through official language test results such as the TEF Canada or TCF Canada, meeting the minimum benchmark thresholds set by IRCC.

The round invited a substantial 5,000 candidates in a single draw — a high volume that signals Canada's continued commitment to attracting Francophone immigrants who can contribute to French-speaking communities outside of Quebec. The CRS cutoff of 399 is notably low, reflecting both the depth of the eligible candidate pool and IRCC's aggressive targets for Francophone immigration in 2026.

What a 399 CRS Cutoff Means for Applicants in the Pool

A CRS cutoff of 399 is a strong indicator of how accessible this particular draw category has become. To put it in perspective, a score of 399 is achievable for many candidates even without the maximum points in every factor. A candidate with moderate Canadian work experience, a completed post-secondary degree, and strong French test scores can often reach this threshold without additional points from a provincial nomination or a job offer.

If you hold an active French-language proficiency Express Entry profile and your CRS score is at or above 399, you should have received an ITA in this draw. If you believe you were eligible but did not receive an invitation, it is critical to verify that your profile is currently active, that your language test results are accurately entered, and that your French proficiency designation is correctly reflected in your profile. Small data entry errors can disqualify an otherwise eligible candidate from a draw.

Trend Context: How Does This Compare to Recent French-Language Draws?

French-language proficiency draws have historically featured lower CRS cutoffs compared to general CRS draws or Canadian Experience Class rounds, largely because the eligible pool is smaller and more targeted. Draw #429 continues this trend, and the 399 cutoff is consistent with — or slightly lower than — what has been observed in recent French-language rounds throughout 2025 and 2026.

The volume of 5,000 ITAs issued in a single round is significant. IRCC has been scaling up the size of French-language draws as part of Canada's broader Francophone immigration strategy, which aims to increase the proportion of French-speaking permanent residents settling outside of Quebec. Drawing 5,000 candidates at once demonstrates that IRCC is prioritizing both volume and accessibility for this category in the current immigration cycle.

Who Specifically Benefits from Draw #429

This draw is most directly beneficial to the following groups:

  • International students who studied in French-language institutions outside of Quebec and have maintained their Express Entry profile with valid French test scores.
  • Workers in Francophone minority communities across provinces such as Ontario, New Brunswick, Manitoba, and Alberta who have accumulated Canadian work experience alongside strong French proficiency.
  • Candidates from French-speaking countries such as France, Morocco, Senegal, Côte d'Ivoire, and Haiti who entered the Express Entry pool with high French test scores but moderate overall CRS scores.
  • Bilingual candidates with strong scores in both English and French who opted to declare French as their primary language, benefiting from the additional CRS points awarded for French proficiency.

For these applicants, a cutoff of 399 represents a realistic and achievable target, particularly when the French-language bonus points built into the CRS system are factored in.

What Applicants Just Below the Cutoff Should Do Right Now

If your CRS score currently sits below 399 and you are targeting French-language proficiency draws, there are concrete steps you can take to improve your position before the next round:

  • Retake your French language test. Improving your TEF Canada or TCF Canada scores — particularly in speaking and writing — can add meaningful CRS points and push your score above future cutoffs.
  • Update your educational credentials. If you have completed additional education since submitting your profile, ensure your credentials are assessed and reflected accurately.
  • Pursue a provincial nomination. Several provinces actively seek Francophone candidates and offer streams that can add 600 CRS points, virtually guaranteeing an ITA in any subsequent draw.
  • Secure a valid job offer. A qualifying job offer from a Canadian employer can add 50 to 200 CRS points depending on the NOC level of the position.
  • Keep your profile active and current. An expired or incomplete profile cannot receive an ITA. Review all entries, including job history, education, and test scores, to ensure everything is accurate and up to date.

French-language draws have been occurring with regularity in 2026, and a cutoff of 399 suggests that candidates with scores in the low-to-mid 400s are well-positioned for upcoming rounds.
